If Aleksei Balabanov’s films were ‘state of the nation’ films, then Russia is in a bad way. Dead Man’s Bluff begins in the present (2005) with a business studies class, the tutor mentioning the opening up of the Russian economy in the 1990s: the film then cuts back to the 1990s. A petty gangster has set up a drugs deal. He sends two henchmen to exchange a case of money for the drugs. A corrupt cop has found out about it and sends three petty gangsters to take the money…but they accidentally take the heroin. The cop makes a deal with another gangster to get rid of the three gangsters he originally hired. But the original two henchman track down the cop and then the three gangsters…then the other gangster and his henchmen turn up. There is a lot of violence in the meetings. We then return to Putin’s Russia and the surviving gangsters are now respectable businessmen. We can see this as an allegory, the Russian market being built on power and violence and corruption…and incompetence. But it doesn’t really work that way in the watching. It is a black comedy where the central characters might be psychopaths, but in a comedy humour outweighs any moral doubts about behaviour. The violence and incompetence and corruption are not despairing, but amusing. Of course, for the film to work we have to be amused and I wasn’t particularly. Last year I watched Brother, but almost didn’t bother when I read it was about a hitman and worried that it was going to be a Russian Pulp Fiction. Thankfully it wasn’t, but Dead Man’s Bluff feels closer to Tarantino…but without the wit and style. Of course, Balabanov has his own style and it has been consistent in the four of his films that I have watched over the past year: a sort of gritty realism. Whether that is Balabanov’s personal style or just one he has slipped into for convenience is debatable…but I’m beginning to think the latter. Maybe there were interests if we gained any sympathy for all the losers and have to watch as they are wiped out, but overall I just found it glib in its easy violence and sick jokes…but maybe I’m a wimp.
