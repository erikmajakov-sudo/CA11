A secretary entered: "Sergey Alexandrovich, you had a call from the Duma. Today's the second reading of the budget. They asked you to be there."

Simon stretched.

"Katya, some tea!" he shouted cheerfully.

"Semyon Evgenievich, exactly five minutes," Katya replied politely.

"Well, deputy, what part of the budget will fall to us?" Simon asked cheerfully.

"Simon, you know I don't take bribes..."

"Oh, oh, so honest... And playing blind man's buff without your little folder, too chicken?"

"I'm sick of you and your folder!" Sergey said angrily. "You'd better pay your taxes, you slacker."

"When I get back from Burkina Faso..."

"But you don't like Negroes!"

"Well... And how long have you been planning to build that church?" he teased Sergey again.

Sergey paused: "I'll build it, want to bet? When oil prices jump again, I'll build it."

"Why should I bet with you, I know prices will jump, and you won't build."

"Oh, where is Russia heading when freaks like you drive around with flashing lights," Sergey said without malice.

"And ones like you don't?"

Sergey remembered something, put his chin on his hands, and said: "You know, Simon, I have a feeling Kaban screwed us over with the heroin back then."

"A thought," said Simon. "But we don't need to kill Kaban."

"I'm not saying we should kill him, I'm just saying he conned us."

"So he conned us, why bring up old stuff..."

The secretary brought tea: "Here you are, Semyon Evgenievich."

"Life's getting hard," said Simon, took the cup, slapped the secretary on the bottom, and looked out the window.
